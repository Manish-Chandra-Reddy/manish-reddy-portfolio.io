How I Think

PM Principles
I Build By

🔍

Problem Before Solution

Every project starts with a crisp problem statement and user segment definition before a single line of code or design. I write Problem Statements as standalone documents to pressure-test the "why" before the "what."

📏

Success Has Numbers

Vague goals lead to vague products. I define quantitative success metrics upfront — conversion rates, engagement rates, time-savings — so product decisions stay honest and accountable to real outcomes.

👥

Specific Users, Not Averages

From gig riders with entry-level Androids in Tier-2 cities, to enterprise sales reps in B2B SaaS — I obsess over the specific human, not the generic persona. Real constraints drive better product decisions.

⚡

Builder Mentality

As an engineer-turned-PM, I build MVPs to validate hypotheses — not just write docs. Shipping Saarthi as a live app, or building an actual Streamlit agent, grounds my product thinking in technical reality.

🌀

AI as Infrastructure

I see GenAI not as a feature, but as the core infrastructure of next-generation products. Each project treats AI agents, semantic analysis, and LLMs as foundational product layers — not bolt-ons.

🎯

Cross-Functional Clarity

My PRDs include target segments, stakeholder maps, KPIs, and Figma prototypes — bridging engineering, design, and business. Good PM work eliminates ambiguity before it becomes a costly misalignment.
